About the Initiative

The Student Digital Access Initiative was created by Dr. Fortem, a Cameroonian lecturer in the United States and IT professional with more than 18 years of experience working with leading global technology companies including Google, Microsoft, and Red Hat.

During multiple visits to Cameroon, he observed that many talented students lack access to computers, reliable internet, and a quiet professional environment for research and study. This initiative was born to change that reality.

Our Mission

To provide students and young graduates with access to technology, internet, research tools, and professional environments that enable academic success and career preparation.

Student Digital Access Partnership Proposal

Initiative: Academic Coworking & Research Center Partnership
Founder: Dr. Fortem – Cameroonian, Lecturer (USA) & IT Professional

This initiative was created by Dr. Fortem, a Cameroonian trainer and lecturer residing in the United States and IT professional with more than 18 years of experience working with leading global technology companies including Google, Microsoft, and Red Hat. During multiple visits to Cameroon, he observed that many talented students lack access to computers, reliable internet, and a quiet professional environment for research and study.
Our coworking and research center provides:
  • Modern computer workstations
  • High‑speed reliable internet
  • Quiet study areas
  • Private research rooms
  • Conference and collaboration spaces
  • Access to digital research resources and online libraries
We invite institutions to partner with us to provide students with discounted access to these resources. This collaboration will support research, digital skills development, and professional preparation. Together, we can bridge the digital gap and empower the next generation of Cameroonian professionals.
